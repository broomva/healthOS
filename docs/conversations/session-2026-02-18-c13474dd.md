> **The Core Legal Question First**
> 
> **Is this a wellness app or a medical app?**
> 
> This single distinction determines almost everything:
> 
> | | Wellness / Consumer Health | Medical / Clinical |
> |---|---|---|
> | Example | Garmin dashboard, sleep tracker | EHR, diagnostic tool, patient portal |
> | HIPAA | ❌ Generally NOT applicable | ✅ Required |
> | FDA regulation | ❌ Generally NOT applicable | ✅ May apply |
> | Liability | Lower | High |
> | Compliance cost | Moderate | Very high |
> 
> If you **never** market it as clinical, diagnostic, or used by healthcare providers — and users are tracking their own wellness data — you're generally in the "wellness app" category. **Language in your marketing matters legally.**
>

> 
> **Regulations You'd Face (by geography)**
> 
> **🇺🇸 United States**
> 
> **HIPAA** — Likely does NOT apply if:
> - You're not a covered entity (hospital, insurer, clearinghouse)
> - You're not a Business Associate processing PHI on behalf of a covered entity
> - Users are consumers managing their own data
> 
> **However**, the FTC Health Breach Notification Rule (2024 update) **does** apply to consumer health apps:
> - Requires notifying users, FTC, and sometimes media if health data is breached
> - Applies even if you're not HIPAA-covered
> - Fines up to $51,744 per violation per day
> 
> **CCPA/CPRA (California)** — Applies if you have CA users AND:
> - 100,000+ consumers/year, OR
> - Derive 50%+ revenue from selling personal data, OR
> - 25,000+ consumers and sell data
> 
> Health data is **"sensitive personal information"** under CPRA:
> - Requires opt-in consent
> - Users can limit use to what's necessary
> - Right to delete, correct, know, opt-out
> 
> **BIPA (Illinois)** — If you collect biometric data (HRV, fingerprint, etc.) from Illinois residents:
> - Explicit written consent required
> - Can't sell biometric data ever
> - $1,000–$5,0
